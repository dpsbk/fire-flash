About WILDFIRE in MAUI

The Maui wildfires in August 2023 were the deadliest in modern U.S. history, killing at least 93 people and destroying over 2,200 homes. The fires also displaced over 4,500 people and caused an estimated $6 billion in damage.The fires started on August 8 and were fueled by dry conditions, high winds, and a buildup of fuel in the brush. The most serious fire, which swept through the historic town of Lahaina, was not contained until August 14.

The Maui wildfires are a wake-up call for the island and for the rest of the United States. They demonstrate the need for a comprehensive approach to fire ecology that includes funding for prevention and mitigation, coordination between agencies, public education, and a culture of fire safety.

The incompetence faced in fire ecology by Maui is a complex issue with many contributing factors.
 Some of the key factors include:
A lack of funding for fire prevention and mitigation measures.
A lack of coordination between local, state, and federal agencies.
A lack of public education about fire safety.
A culture of complacency in the face of fire danger.
